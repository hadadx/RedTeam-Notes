Kerberoasting

Kerberoasting is a Kerberos attack that targets service accounts (SPNs). An authenticated domain user requests a TGS ticket for a service, extracts the encrypted ticket, and performs offline password cracking to recover the service account's password.

How It Works

  1. The attacker identifies service accounts with registered SPNs.
  2. A TGS is requested from the Domain Controller.
  3. The returned ticket is encrypted using the service account's password-derived key.
  4. The ticket is cracked offline to recover the plaintext password.

Requirements

  • Valid domain user credentials (TGT).
  • One or more service accounts with registered SPNs.
  • Network access to the Domain Controller.

Failure Conditions

  • Strong, long service account passwords.
  • Modern Kerberos encryption (AES) instead of RC4 where applicable.
  • No accessible or vulnerable SPNs.

Usage Scenarios

  • Targeting service accounts with elevated privileges.
  • During internal Active Directory assessments after obtaining a valid domain user account.
  • Identifying weak service account passwords for privilege escalation or lateral movement.
